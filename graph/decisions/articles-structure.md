# Articles of Association: Structure and Company Name

## Context

Drafting the Articles of Association for Sun Turtle Internet raised two questions: whether to include a specific objects clause, and what name to use.

## Decision 1: No objects clause

The initial draft included a detailed Part 2 (Objects) defining the company's purpose — ISP services, environmental responsibility, AI-forward operations, digital inclusion. Under the Companies Act 2006 this is unnecessary for a CLG.

The standard Companies House model articles for CLGs intentionally contain no objects clause. Under modern UK company law:

- A company has unrestricted objects by default
- You only include restricted objects if you want to constrain what the company can do
- Model articles omit them deliberately

For Sun Turtle Internet, objects are **not included**. The company can engage in any lawful activity.

Objects ARE still relevant when:
- **Charities** — the Charity Commission expects specific charitable purposes and public benefit wording
- **CICs** — need a defined community purpose and asset lock
- **Membership organisations** — to limit activities and prevent mission drift
- **Grant-funded non-profits** — some grant-makers require constitutional purposes

None of these apply. No objects clause.

## Decision 2: "Limited" in the company name

A CLG must end with "Limited" (or "Ltd") unless exempt. Exemption is only available for charitable companies. Sun Turtle Internet is not a charity.

**Company name**: Sun Turtle Internet Limited

Trading names can differ — the legal name "Limited" has no bearing on branding.

## Impact

- Articles are shorter (objects clause removed)
- Directors have flexibility to pursue any lawful activity without amending the articles
- Company name on all legal documents is "Sun Turtle Internet Limited"
- Customer-facing brand can use a trading name
