# Articles of Association: Structure and Company Name

## Context

Drafting the Articles of Association for Sun Turtle Internet raised two questions: whether to include a specific objects clause, and what name to use.

## Decision 1: Objects clause (included after all)

The initial draft included a detailed Part 2 (Objects) — ISP services, environmental responsibility, digital inclusion. Under the Companies Act 2006 this is unnecessary for a CLG (unrestricted objects is the default), and the initial decision was to omit it.

However, on reflection we decided to **include** an objects clause. The driver: as a CLG with a social/environmental mission baked into the brand, having explicit objects acts as a constitutional anchor — it signals intent to members, wholesale partners, and regulators. It also aligns with the "community benefit" spirit even though Sun Turtle is not a CIC or charity.

The clause was drafted broadly enough (sustainable/socially conscious business, connectivity, digital inclusion, open-source tools) to avoid constraining the directors, with an entrenched provision requiring >50% member attendance at a special resolution to modify it.

## Decision 2: "Limited" in the company name

A CLG must end with "Limited" (or "Ltd") unless exempt. Exemption is only available for charitable companies. Sun Turtle Internet is not a charity.

**Company name**: Sun Turtle Internet Limited

Trading names can differ — the legal name "Limited" has no bearing on branding.

## Completion

- **2026-05-08**: Incorporation application submitted to Companies House for Sun Turtle Internet Limited (CLG). Awaiting acceptance and certificate of incorporation.

## Impact

- Articles include objects clause — broadly drafted, with entrenched amendment provision
- Company name on all legal documents is "Sun Turtle Internet Limited"
- Customer-facing brand can use a trading name
