# Domain: FreeThought and `suninternet.co.uk`

## Context

The company needs a domain for email, website, and supplier credibility. Choices: go with a mainstream registrar (GoDaddy, 123-Reg, Namecheap) or an ethical alternative. The CLG structure and values-driven positioning push toward alignment across every vendor touchpoint.

## Decision

Register `suninternet.co.uk` at **[FreeThought](https://www.freethought.uk/about/)** — a UK-based ethical domain registrar.

## Rationale

- **Alignment**: FreeThought explicitly operates on ethical principles — no upselling, no dark patterns, transparent pricing. This matches Sun Turtle's "values-driven, not marketed" posture.
- **UK-based**: .uk domain specialist with UK support, relevant for a UK ISP.
- **Simplicity**: Clean interface, no upsell noise. Same ethos as the rest of the stack (CLG, green positioning).
- **`suninternet.co.uk`**: Short, memorable, clearly an internet company. `suninternet` is the brandable face; the legal entity remains "Sun Turtle Internet Limited." The `.co.uk` TLD signals UK domicile — important for wholesale partner credibility.

## Alternatives

- **GoDaddy / 123-Reg / Namecheap**: Cheaper, but misaligned — mass-market, upsell-heavy, no ethical positioning. Undercuts the brand.
- **Gandi**: French ethical registrar. Strong, but no UK-specific advantage over FreeThought.
- **`sunturtleinternet.co.uk`**: Matches legal name exactly, but clunky for customers to type. `suninternet` is cleaner for branding.
- **`.uk` vs `.co.uk`**: `.co.uk` is more recognisable to UK consumers than the newer `.uk`.

## Impact

- Domain is the foundation for email (Google Workspace / Fastmail), website, and supplier onboarding
- Ethical registrar choice reinforces brand consistency
- `suninternet` as trading name vs `Sun Turtle Internet Limited` as legal name — clean separation
