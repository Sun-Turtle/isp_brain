# Company Structure: Company Limited by Guarantee

## Context

Sun Turtle Internet needs a legal structure. Three options considered: standard Private Limited Company (Ltd), Community Interest Company (CIC), and Company Limited by Guarantee (CLG). The philosophy of the venture — AI-first, values-driven, "friendly pirates" — influenced the choice.

## Decision

Incorporate as a **Company Limited by Guarantee (CLG)** — the same structure used by Green ISP (founded 2003, Hebden Bridge, ~2 staff).

No shareholders, no share capital. Profits reinvested. Members (founder + co-founders) each guarantee £1 on winding up.

## Rationale

- CLG structurally matches the ethos — not-for-profit by design, not by marketing
- No legal duty to maximise shareholder value (unlike a standard Ltd)
- No second regulator overhead (unlike a CIC — CIC Regulator adds ongoing reporting)
- Green ISP proves the model: 22 years, Ethical Consumer Best Buy 4 times, running on Openreach BT Wholesale
- Startup capital goes in as a director's loan, repaid from surplus

## Alternatives

- **Standard Ltd**: flexible, well understood, but directors have a legal duty to chase profit for shareholders. The structure contradicts a mission-driven brand.
- **CIC**: mission-locked with asset lock. But adds the CIC Regulator as a second regulatory body — overhead a 5-person ISP doesn't need.
- **Partnership / Sole Trader**: no limited liability. Unsuitable for a consumer-facing ISP with Ofcom and data protection exposure.

## Impact

- No equity investment path — revenue funds growth
- Founder capital via director's loan
- Members (not shareholders) have ultimate control
- Strong ethical positioning with consumers
- Proven template from Green ISP
