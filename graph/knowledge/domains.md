# The Five Domains

## Identity — Who are we?

The source. Ground truths — legal structure, ownership, mission. Everything else in the graph refers back here.

## Knowledge — What do we need to know?

The accumulation. Facts, rules, context, research, procedures. Not *what we have* but *what we understand*. Never a proxy for resource content — provide labelling and relationships.

## Decisions — What did we choose and why?

The scars. A structural choice or trade-off with alternatives considered. Append-only: superseded decisions link to their replacement rather than being rewritten.

## Tasks — What are we doing?

The friction between thinking and doing. Every task should trace back to knowledge (the requirement) or a decision (the authorisation). A task that links to nothing is orphaned intent.

## Resources — What do we have?

The inventory. Tools, people, money, contracts, repos, infrastructure. Describes what something is and where to find it. Context about *why* it matters belongs in knowledge.

## Relationship

Identity grounds everything → Knowledge informs Decisions → Decisions create Tasks → Tasks use Resources.

If a file fits two domains, split it or write a decision explaining the choice.
