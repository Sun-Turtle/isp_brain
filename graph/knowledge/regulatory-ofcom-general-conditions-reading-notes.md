# Ofcom General Conditions Reading Notes

- communications providers do not need a license (--> this is aligned to EU Frameworks)
- they need to meet 3 types of conditions:
    - network functioning conditions (A)
    - equipment/technical numbering and conditions (B)
    - consumer protection conditions (C)

### A
- Communications Providers must comply with compulsory standards/specifications listed in Article 17 of Framework Directive and Article 39 of EECC Directive (European Electronic Communications Code Directive 2018)
- 'Regulated Providers' (who are they? includes ISPs?) must provide fullest access of internet in case of emergency or force majeure (how can this be guaranteed for fibre?)

## B
- nothing really... mainly about phone numbers, quite dated

## C
- you MUST clearly communicate your core subscription price and price change information before signing contract with consumer and on a DURABLE electronic/physical medium (paper, PDF etc)
- you cannot renew a contract with a consumer unless they have given their express consent to do so
- contract terms can be no more than 24mo
- you must allow consumers to subscribe for min 12mo
- you must give at least 1mo notice for any contract changes
- contract terminations must be free of charge
- must send an annual 'best tariff' notification each 12mo
- tariff information must include information on:
    - termination charges
    - equipment
    - handheld locking restrictions (not relevant i think)
    - additional charges
    - maintenance/setup charges
- you cant charge for making a bill to a consumer
- you must retain billing information for at least 12mo
- consumer must have access to their own billing
- you must give warning before debt collection, and not discriminate
- complaints handling must comply with OFCOM APPROVED COMPLAINTS CODE --> this is included in 'Annex to Condition C4'
- you must be a member of an ALTERNATIVE DISPUTE RESOLUTION (ADR) scheme
- you must have policies for consumers with vulnerabilities and publish them
- you must maintain a EFFICIENT and SIMPLE process for Communications Provider Migration (i.e. switching), including for bundles
- must keep records for 12mo when a consumer switches
- new provider must request a consumers' details from the old provider
- if you fail to meet requirements during switching of providers, you must provide compensation
