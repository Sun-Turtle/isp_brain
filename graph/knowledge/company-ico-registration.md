# ICO Registration (Data Protection Fee)

Under the Data Protection (Charges and Information) Regulations 2018, every organisation that processes personal data must pay an annual data protection fee to the ICO, unless exempt. An ISP is not exempt.

## Why mandatory for ISPs

As a communications provider you process:
- Customer name, address, contact details (billing + service provisioning)
- Payment information
- IP address assignment logs
- Traffic data (even if not inspected)
- Support interaction records

This makes you a data controller under UK GDPR. The fee funds the ICO's regulatory work.

## Fee tier

For a small reseller ISP (≤10 staff, ≤£632k turnover):

| Tier | Annual fee | Applies to |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (micro) | £40 | Most small ISPs at launch |
| Tier 2 (SME) | £60 | 11-249 staff or £632k-£36m turnover |
| Tier 3 (large) | £2,900 | 250+ staff or £36m+ turnover |

Sun Turtle starts at Tier 1 (£40/year). Payable by direct debit or card. Auto-renewing.

## Registration process

1. Go to [ico.org.uk/fee-checker](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/data-protection-fee/self-assessment/)
2. Complete the self-assessment (determines your tier)
3. Pay the fee online
4. Receive registration number — add to website privacy policy

Takes ~15 minutes. Registration is public — you appear on the [ICO register of fee payers](https://ico.org.uk/about-the-ico/what-we-do/register-of-fee-payers/).

## Exemptions (not applicable)

Some organisations are exempt (e.g. processing only for staff admin, accounts, or not using automated systems). An ISP processes customer data for service delivery — no exemption applies.

## Relationship with UK GDPR compliance

Paying the fee is the minimum. You must also:
- Maintain a privacy policy (see [Core Admin Stack](company-core-admin-stack.md))
- Have a lawful basis for processing (contractual necessity for service provisioning)
- Respond to subject access requests within 1 month
- Report personal data breaches to ICO within 72 hours if high risk
- Appoint a data protection officer (unlikely required for a micro ISP unless large-scale monitoring)

## When to register

Immediately after incorporation, before collecting any customer data. If you take even one pre-registration email enquiry, you're processing personal data.
