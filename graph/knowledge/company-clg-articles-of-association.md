# CLG Articles of Association

The articles of association are the constitutional document of the company — the rulebook defining how it's governed. Filed with Companies House during incorporation.

Gov.uk provides free model articles for CLGs. You can use these as-is or customise them. Most small CLGs use the model articles with minor changes.

## Key clauses a CLG must include

### Guarantee clause

Each member guarantees to pay a nominal amount if the company is wound up insolvent. Typically £1. This is what makes it "limited by guarantee."

Example (from BUPA's articles, `resources/bupa-articles-of-association.md`):
> The liability of each member is limited to £1, being the amount that each member undertakes to contribute to the assets of the company in the event of its being wound up.

### No share capital

The articles must explicitly state the company has no share capital. This prevents creating shares accidentally. Combined with the guarantee clause, this defines the CLG.

### Objects clause

What the company exists to do. For a CLG, the objects define permitted activities. All surplus must be applied towards these objects.

Sun Turtle's objects would include:
- Providing internet services to the public
- Operating as a communications provider
- Operating in environmentally responsible ways

### Members

Who the members are, how they're appointed, what rights they have, and how they can be removed. Key points:
- Members appoint and remove directors
- Members approve changes to articles
- Members approve the accounts at AGMs
- No requirement on number of members (can be 1)
- No requirement that employees or customers are members

### Directors

How directors are appointed, removed, and what powers they have. Key points:
- Directors manage day-to-day operations
- Minimum 1 director
- Directors can be members (typically are)
- Directors can receive salary (this is not a dividend)

### Decision-making

How decisions are made — board meetings, general meetings, written resolutions, quorum requirements, voting thresholds.

### Distribution of assets on winding up

If the company is dissolved, any remaining assets must be transferred to another organisation with similar objects — not distributed to members. This is the "not-for-profit" guarantee.

## Model articles from gov.uk

Gov.uk publishes model articles for companies limited by guarantee (Companies Act 2006). Free to use. Download from gov.uk/guidance/model-articles.

### Latest version: for companies incorporated on or after 28 April 2013

These are the current defaults and what Sun Turtle would use.

The BUPA articles (`resources/bupa-articles-of-association.md`) are a real-world example — 39 pages, adopted by special resolution in 2021. More detailed than the model articles but a useful reference for a mature CLG.

## What Sun Turtle needs to customise

Using the model articles with these additions:
1. Objects clause specifying ISP/communications activities
2. Optional: environmental and ethical commitments in objects (not legally enforced unless stated, but signals intent)
3. Member guarantee amount (£1 is standard)
4. Director appointment/removal terms (for a small team, keep it simple)
