# Ofcom Notification (General Authorisation)

Under the UK's general authorisation regime, communications providers do not apply for a licence — they operate under the General Conditions of Entitlement and must notify Ofcom. This is a registration, not an approval.

## Legal basis

Communications Act 2003, sections 33-35. The general conditions apply automatically when you provide electronic communications services. Notification is a requirement under those conditions — not a prerequisite to being bound by them.

## What notification does

- Registers your company on Ofcom's communications provider database
- Issues a Communications Provider ID (CP ID) — needed by some wholesalers
- Ensures you're on the radar for regulatory updates and consultations
- Allows Ofcom to contact you for compliance matters

It does NOT:
- Grant you a licence
- Approve your business
- Constitute permission to operate (you already have that under general authorisation)

## When to notify

After you have:
1. Incorporated company ✓
2. Business bank account
3. Core admin stack (domain, privacy policy, complaints procedure)
4. Clarity on service scope (fixed broadband, VoIP, etc.)

Do it before approaching wholesalers — some require your CP ID during onboarding. Real timeline: 4-6 weeks before you need wholesale agreements signed.

## Process

1. Complete the Ofcom notification form via their portal
2. Provide: company name, number, registered address, director details, contact information
3. Specify service types (internet access, possibly VoIP)
4. Submit — typically processed within 2-4 weeks

No fee. Not a complex form. The challenge is understanding which conditions apply (see [Ofcom General Conditions](regulatory-ofcom-general-conditions.md)).

## Service categories for a reseller ISP

Typical selections at notification:
- Fixed internet access (broadband)
- Electronic communications service (the umbrella category)
- VoIP (if offering voice — adds emergency services obligations)

Do NOT select categories you don't provide. Over-declaring triggers obligations you don't want.

## Post-notification obligations

Once notified, you're actively on the regulatory radar. Must remain compliant with:
- General Conditions (consumer contracts, complaints, switching, vulnerability)
- Annual turnover reporting to Ofcom (if revenue exceeds threshold)
- Participation in Ofcom consultations and market reviews
- Compliance with requests for information under section 135

## Difference from a "licence"

| Aspect | General authorisation (UK) | Licence regime (many countries) |
|---|---|---|
| Application | Notification only | Formal application with business plan |
| Approval | None — automatic | Discretionary, can be refused |
| Cost | Free | Often substantial fees |
| Conditions | Standard GCs, apply to all | Individual, negotiated |
| Revocation | Breach of conditions | Licence expiry or breach |

The UK model is designed to lower barriers to entry. Use it.
