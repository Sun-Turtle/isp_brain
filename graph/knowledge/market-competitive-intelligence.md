# Market Landscape

## Lead UK references

These two are useful north stars for "small team, high automation, technical honesty" — exactly the lane an AI-first reseller would want to study (from the outside).

### Andrews & Arnold (A&A)

| Dimension        | Notes                                                                                |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Positioning      | Famous for no-nonsense, tech-literate service; IPv6, static IPs, geeks welcome       |
| Why they matter  | Proof that a niche ISP can win on trust + capability without looking like BT adverts |
| Pattern to steal | Automation, clear docs, community/forum energy, low traditional marketing            |


### Aquiss

| Dimension        | Notes                                                       |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Positioning      | Smaller UK ISP, lean footprint, broadband-first focus       |
| Why they matter  | Proof that non-huge brands can stay viable with focused ops |
| Pattern to steal | Efficient ops, loyal niche, avoid spray-and-pray TV ads     |


Not claiming their exact headcount or stack — treat them as cultural and strategic references.

## Other EU/UK stories

Other EU/UK "tiny ISP" stories exist; for this doc, A&A and Aquiss are the lead pair.
