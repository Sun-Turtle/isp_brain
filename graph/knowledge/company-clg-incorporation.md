# CLG Incorporation Process

Incorporating a CLG is the same process as a standard Ltd — £12 online, 24-48 hours. The process creates the legal entity and makes it real.

## Requirements

- Unique company name (check Companies House availability first)
- Registered office address in England & Wales (can be a home address or agent)
- At least one director (at least 16, not disqualified)
- At least one member (guarantor) — typically the same person as the director
- Articles of Association (see [separate doc](company-clg-articles-of-association.md))
- Memorandum of Association (standard template, signed by members)
- IN01 form filed online

## Cost

- £12 standard online incorporation (£40 by post)
- Formation agents offer packages from £10-100 (handle paperwork, provide registered address)
- Free model articles from gov.uk
- No separate regulator fee (unlike CIC)

## Timeline

| Step | Time |
|---|---|
| Choose name, prepare docs | 1-2 hours |
| File IN01 online | 10 minutes |
| Companies House approval | 24-48 hours |
| Total | ~2 days |
| Certificate of Incorporation arrives | Same day as approval |

## Post-incorporation

- Open a business bank account (some banks don't understand CLGs but it's manageable)
- Register for Corporation Tax with HMRC within 3 months
- Issue share certificates — not applicable (no shares). Issue membership certificates if desired.
- Hold first board meeting, adopt articles, appoint directors, record minutes
- Register with ICO for data protection (£40-60/year)
- Register for VAT if approaching £90k threshold
