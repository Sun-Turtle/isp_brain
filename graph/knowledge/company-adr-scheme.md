# ADR Scheme Membership

Consumer-facing communications providers in the UK must belong to an Ofcom-approved Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) scheme. This is one of the first real compliance costs and a hard requirement under the General Conditions.

## Legal requirement

General Condition C4 (complaints and dispute resolution) requires providers to:
1. Have a published complaints procedure
2. Be a member of an Ofcom-approved ADR scheme
3. Inform customers of their right to escalate to ADR after 8 weeks or on deadlock

Failure to comply: Ofcom can issue a section 96A notification and ultimately fine up to 10% of turnover.

## The two Ofcom-approved ADR schemes

### CISAS (CEDR)

Communication & Internet Services Adjudication Scheme. Run by CEDR (Centre for Effective Dispute Resolution). Covers most independent ISPs including AAISP, Aquiss, IDNet, and Green ISP.

- **Cost**: Annual membership fee based on size (~£300-600/year for micro ISPs) plus case fees per dispute (~£100-300 per case escalated to adjudication)
- **Process**: Adjudication — CISAS issues a binding decision on the company
- **Turnaround**: ~6-8 weeks from complaint acceptance
- **Consumer cost**: Free

### Communications Ombudsman

Alternative to CISAS. Free ADR membership for ISPA members with turnover under £20m.

- **Cost**: Free to join (funded by industry + case fees)
- **Process**: Ombudsman-style — investigation leading to binding resolution
- **Consumer cost**: Free

## Which to choose

| Factor | CISAS | Comm Ombudsman |
|---|---|---|
| Setup cost | Annual fee from day 1 | Free with ISPA membership |
| Industry familiarity | Known by small ISPs | Growing, ISPA partner |
| ISPA requirement | Not required | Requires ISPA membership |
| Green ISP uses | CISAS | — |

**Recommendation for Sun Turtle**: Join ISPA (for industry credibility and policy access), then use the free Comm Ombudsman membership that comes with it. Two birds, one membership fee.

## ISPA membership as ADR path

ISPA membership (£550-950/year for small ISPs) includes:
- Free Comm Ombudsman membership
- Use of ISPA logo (credibility)
- Regulatory updates and policy briefings
- Industry networking and events
- Representation to Ofcom and government

This bundles ADR compliance + industry credibility + regulatory intelligence into one cost.

## Process timeline

1. Join ISPA (~2 weeks processing)
2. ADR membership is activated through ISPA (Comm Ombudsman) or applied separately (CISAS)
3. Add ADR details to:
   - Website (footer and complaints page)
   - Complaints procedure
   - Customer contracts
   - Deadlock/8-week letters

## Consumer-level process

When a customer complaint escalates:
1. Customer raises complaint with ISP
2. ISP has 8 weeks to resolve
3. If unresolved or deadlocked, ISP issues a "deadlock letter" signposting to ADR
4. Customer submits complaint to ADR scheme (free)
5. ADR investigates and issues binding decision
6. ISP must comply with the decision

Case fees are only incurred when a complaint reaches the ADR for adjudication. Well-handled internal complaints = fewer case fees.
